### 1. **waste_ratio** – Environmental & Operational Risk Proxy
**Why it matters in mining**  
Mining companies must report waste-to-ore ratios for ESG compliance, permits, and cost control. A high ratio means more environmental impact and higher operating costs.

**How it is calculated**  
\[
\text{waste_ratio} = \frac{\text{waste_tonnes}}{\text{production_tonnes}}
\]

**Code snippet from your ETL**
```python
df['production_tonnes'] = df['value_tonnes'].fillna(0)
df['waste_tonnes'] = df['value_tonnes_waste'].fillna(0)

df['waste_ratio'] = df['waste_tonnes'] / df['production_tonnes'].replace(0, float('nan'))
```

### 2. **efficiency_score** – Simple Performance Metric
**Why it matters**  
A single number that tells operations teams how efficiently a mine converts waste into saleable product. Higher score = better mine.

**How it is calculated**  
\[
\text{efficiency_score} = \frac{1}{\text{waste_ratio} + 1}
\]

**Code snippet**
```python
df['efficiency_score'] = 1 / (df['waste_ratio'] + 1)
```

### 3. **risk_level** – Red/Yellow/Green Flag for Management
**Why it matters**  
Mining executives and regulators use risk categories to prioritize sites for audits or investment.

**How it is calculated**  
We use pandas `pd.cut` to bucket the waste_ratio:

| waste_ratio range | risk_level |
|-------------------|------------|
| 0 – 0.5           | Low        |
| 0.5 – 1.5         | Medium     |
| > 1.5             | High       |

**Code snippet**
```python
df['risk_level'] = pd.cut(
    df['waste_ratio'],
    bins=[0, 0.5, 1.5, float('inf')],
    labels=['Low', 'Medium', 'High']
)
```

### 4. **yoy_growth** – Year-over-Year Production Trend
**Why it matters**  
Shows whether a mine is growing, stable, or declining – critical for reserve forecasting and investor reporting.

**How it is calculated**  
\[
\text{yoy_growth (\%)} = \frac{\text{production_tonnes}_{\text{current year}} - \text{production_tonnes}_{\text{previous year}}}{\text{production_tonnes}_{\text{previous year}}} \times 100
\]

**Code snippet**
```python
df = df.sort_values(['mine_id', 'year'])                    # important: sort first
df['yoy_growth'] = df.groupby('mine_id')['production_tonnes'].pct_change() * 100
```
